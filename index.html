I am an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at Furman University.

I received my PhD in 2022 from Stony Brook University advised by Dr. Steven Skiena and Dr. Lilianne Mujica Parodi.

My research interests lie at the intersection of data science, machine learning, computational neuroscience and computational social science. For a longer biography, please see my Furman faculty page.

Research ^

The following is a list of active research projects I am working on:

   Visualizing Dynamic Networks

I am currently working on extending Hierarchical Edge Bundling and Force-Directed edge bundling for dynamic graphs towards mechanistic interpretability of artificial neural networks as well as for improved diagnostics of time-varying biological functional connectivity networks.

   Improving Participation and Success of Underrepresented Students in Computer Science

I am currently working on better understanding and improving participation and success of underrepresented students in Computer Science by mining data from Integrated Post-Secondary Education System (IPEDS).
